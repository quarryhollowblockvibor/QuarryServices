Diorite is a sedimentary rock that forms when igneous dikes intrude the overlying sedimentary layers and create fractures that allow molten magma to break through. Diorites are typically rich in feldspars, quartz, and hornblende minerals that make it a red to reddish-brown to black colored rock with well developed cleavage.
